# Single-Focus Pattern

## When to Use

This pattern works best when:
- **One major announcement or event** generates multiple reactions/analyses
- **Central controversial topic** spawns commentary from different angles
- **Official release** accompanied by community response and critical analysis
- The theme has a clear **"main character"** article with supporting voices

**Example scenarios:**
- New AI model release (GPT-5, Claude Opus, etc.) + analysis threads
- Major product announcement (GitHub Copilot Workspace) + developer reactions
- Controversial paper/blog post + rebuttals and discussions
- Breaking industry news + expert commentary

## Structure

```
1. Lead Article (primary source/announcement)
   ↓
2. Supporting Article #1 (technical perspective)
   ↓
3. Supporting Article #2 (business/impact perspective)
   ↓
4. Supporting Article #3 (community/developer perspective)
   ↓
5. Optional: Contrarian/skeptical view (if exists)
```

**Key principle:** Establish the "what" first, then explore the "so what" and "now what"

## Assembly Guidelines

### Article Selection & Ordering

1. **Lead position**: Most authoritative/official source
   - Official announcement, paper, or primary source
   - Sets the factual foundation
   - Answers: "What happened?"

2. **Supporting positions**: Ordered by:
   - Technical depth (deeper insights first)
   - Perspective diversity (alternate viewpoints)
   - Narrative progression (build to most thought-provoking)

3. **Final position**: Most provocative or future-looking
   - Challenges assumptions
   - Raises unanswered questions
   - Points to implications

### What to Emphasize

- **Clarity of the central topic**: Reader should understand "the thing" quickly
- **Diversity of reactions**: Show multiple valid perspectives
- **Evolution of understanding**: From initial announcement to deeper implications
- **Unanswered questions**: What remains uncertain or controversial

### Common Pitfalls

- ❌ Treating all articles as equal weight (leads to confusion)
- ❌ Burying the lead (put main announcement first)
- ❌ Grouping similar perspectives together (creates echo chamber)
- ❌ Ignoring contrarian views (reduces intellectual honesty)

## Narrative Flow

### Introduction Strategy

Establish the central event/topic clearly:

**Template approach:**
```
[Event/announcement] が [date/context] に発表され、
[field/community] に大きな波紋を広げている。
[Brief summary of the main point]
```

**Example:**
```
OpenAI が GPT-5 を突如発表し、AI 開発者コミュニティに
衝撃を与えた。マルチモーダル性能の飛躍的向上が
注目される一方、安全性への懸念も浮上している。
```

### Transition Strategies

**Moving from lead to first reaction:**
```
この発表を受け、[perspective] の視点から [analyst] が分析している。
→ "この発表を受け、技術面から Andrej Karpathy が深掘りしている。"
```

**Moving between different perspectives:**
```
一方で、[different angle] からは [different conclusion] が示されている。
→ "一方で、ビジネス面からは収益化の難しさが指摘されている。"
```

**Introducing contrarian view:**
```
しかし、[skeptic/critic] は [contrarian point] と警鐘を鳴らす。
→ "しかし、Gary Marcus は誇大広告に過ぎないと警鐘を鳴らす。"
```

### Conclusion Strategy

Synthesize what the diversity of reactions reveals:

**Questions to answer:**
1. What does the range of reactions tell us about the significance?
2. What remains uncertain or unresolved?
3. What should readers watch for next?

**Template approach:**
```
[Main topic] をめぐる議論は、[what the debate reveals]。
[Key takeaway from synthesis]。
今後注目すべきは [what to watch]。
```

**Example:**
```
GPT-5 をめぐる議論は、AI の性能向上だけでなく
社会的影響への備えの重要性を浮き彫りにしている。
技術的ブレークスルーと倫理的課題が並走する現状で、
今後注目すべきは規制と技術革新のバランスだ。
```

## Example from Past Journals

### Journal 2025-12-27: Theme "GitHub Copilot Workspace の衝撃"

**Pattern used:** Single-Focus

**Article structure:**
1. **Lead**: GitHub 公式発表 (Copilot Workspace とは何か)
2. **Technical reaction**: Developer の実践レポート (使用感、性能)
3. **Business perspective**: VC による市場インパクト分析
4. **Critical view**: "Copilot は開発者を置き換えるのか" 議論

**Why it worked:**
- 公式発表で基礎を固めた後、多角的な反応を提示
- 楽観論と懐疑論をバランス良く配置
- 最後の記事で "これは何を意味するのか" を問い直し

**Narrative flow:**
- Intro: Workspace の登場が開発フローを変える可能性
- Transitions: "この発表に開発者は..." → "一方、投資家は..." → "しかし、本質的な問いは..."
- Conclusion: ツールの進化だけでなく、開発者の役割再定義が本題

## Customization Examples

### Variation 1: Announcement-Light Version

When the "lead article" is weak or missing:

**Modified structure:**
1. **Synthesis intro**: Editor が複数ソースから主題を構成
2. Supporting articles in standard order
3. Conclusion pointing to missing official statement

**Use case:** 噂や非公式情報が先行し、公式発表が後から来る場合

### Variation 2: Multi-Event Single-Focus

When multiple related events create one narrative:

**Modified structure:**
1. **Event A** (primary trigger)
2. **Event B** (related/response)
3. **Analysis**: How A and B connect
4. **Implications**: What the pattern means

**Use case:** OpenAI の動き → Google の対抗発表 → 競争激化の分析

### Variation 3: Deep-Dive Single-Focus

When one article is comprehensive enough to stand alone:

**Modified structure:**
1. **Main article** (comprehensive coverage)
2. **Supplementary articles** (specific aspects not covered)
3. **Optional**: Editor commentary on what's still missing

**Use case:** State of AI Report 2025 のような大型レポート

## Decision Checklist

Use Single-Focus pattern when:

- [ ] There is ONE clear central topic/event
- [ ] Multiple sources discuss THE SAME thing from different angles
- [ ] Hierarchy exists (one source is more authoritative)
- [ ] Reactions/commentary add value beyond the original

**Do NOT use** when:
- [ ] Articles discuss related but distinct topics
- [ ] No clear "main" article exists
- [ ] All perspectives are equally authoritative
- [ ] Articles build on each other sequentially

→ Consider **Multi-Perspective** or **Progressive-Sequence** instead

## Assembly Prompts for STEP_08

When using this pattern, answer these questions:

1. **What is THE central event/topic** this theme addresses?
2. **Why is the lead article the lead?** (What makes it authoritative?)
3. **What progression do reactions follow?** (Technical → Business → Cultural?)
4. **What tension exists** between different perspectives?
5. **What does the diversity of reactions reveal** about the topic's significance?
6. **What remains unresolved** after all perspectives are heard?
